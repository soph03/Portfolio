Overview

Introduction

EA is one of the largest gaming companies in the world, including games such as Apex Legends, The Sims 4, and It Takes Two. During my 4 month internship, I worked on the EA App, a PC platform for players to download and manage their games, as well as connect with other players across platforms.

Project

As a UX Design Intern, I discovered problems, created new game states and simplified the game tile system. Throughout the process, I worked with my Manager Dave, gaining experience presenting, building rationale, and practicing product thinking.

Problem

Game tiles are presented at the same level of importance in the discovery setting.

Context

EA Application

The EA App is a windows application for players to download, update and manage their games. It provides the ability to connect with friends and integrate with external applications such as discord.

Throughout the application, game tiles can be found displaying essential information such as the image of the game, price, and membership type.

Design Process

Problem Discovery

Pt 1. Problem identification

To familiarize myself with game tiles and application, I conducted an audit to identify the different types of tiles in the app and read through previous user research reports.

I sorted the tiles into groups (user journey, location, tile size, and archetype motivations) to identify potential inconsistencies and gaps.

Pt 2. Problem prioritization

The problems we identified with my manager were sorted and organized from highest impact to least effort to determine which problems to focus on.

Focus Areas

How might we...

  1. Present different game states?

  2. Place price and discounts more effectively?

  3. Re-imagine a consistent Add-on tile design?

User Research

Leading question: How might we present different game states?

Current Design

On the landing page of the EA app, the game tile and the DLC tile carry the same components, except the DLC is differentiated through a grey badge.

Competitive Analysis

With the problem space in mind, I wanted to understand how other competitors presented different states, the use of the content, as well as how tiles are differentiated between games and DLC (game add-ons).

Findings

  • States identified through badges, banners, or labels
  • DLC identified within an image, title, secondary text, or badges
  • Some apps have categorized/filtered pages: new, top seller, top-rated, upcoming, etc

Design Space

How might we present different game states while leveraging and minimizing existing design systems?

Existing Designs

Current Design

A game tile consists of multiple components. Since we want to use utilize the current design system and create minimal change, the components I want to leverage are:

  • Secondary badge
  • Secondary text
  • Label

Design Considerations

Copy Ideas

For copy inspiration and ideas, I learned the user journey of a player, analyzed the states used by competitors, and referred to states that are in the process of development.

After discussing with my Manager, the game states we chose to focus on were: Coming soon, Pre-orderable, New, Discount percentage (ex. -30%), and In-collection.

Information Hierarchy

Although the position of the badge and the secondary text is fixed, there is some flexibility on where we want to place the tag. Through some exploring, having the 'New' label placed in front of the game title was the best fit because it provided separation between the other labels within the tile, a clear hierarchy and does not affect the title when the text wraps.

Visual Hierarchy

Due to sales being the main driver of revenue, the discount state respectively leads in the visual hierarchy, followed by the 'New' state, where I learned from user interviews and competitive analysis that new games are a key motivator for players. Lastly, we have the rest of the states incorporating a muted visual.

Design Revisions

First Draft

A few designs were brought to the greater design team and it was agreed that this design could be pursued further because the separation between the badges and labels helped create a stronger visual hierarchy.

However, one issue that we encountered is the visual noise produced by the 4 different labels within the add-on tile.

Follow up

How can we minimize the design of the add-on tile?

First design change

Remove strike-through price.

Second design change

Remove Add-on labels and differentiate them through shape and shorter tile size.

Third design change

Replace pre-orderable with coming soon.

Fourth design change

Create a system and hierarchy where only one game state appears at a time

Game States Final Design

Brief

Currently, game titles are presented at the same level of importance/hierarchy. This creates a design opportunity to draw increased attention to specific titles in a discovery setting that occupies a desirable or contextually useful status for the user.

What is a game state?

A certain stage within the life cycle of a game product. The game state that we have designed are split into two groups.

Success criteria

  • Users clear distinguish tiles with states and those without
  • Users can differentiate between game tiles and add-ons
  • Reduce complexity within the game tile system
  • Leverage existing design components

Final Polish

Goals of game state:

To Attract

Unreleased Game

Before the release of a game, there are two states: Coming soon and Pre-orderable.

Unreleased games are denoted with a 'Coming soon' label and if they are orderable, a price tag is included in addition to the label.

New Game

When a game is released, the game tile would include the 'New' label to notify and attract players.

Existing Game

On special occasions and holidays, EA offers games at a discounted price. During this time, selected games will have a discounted percentage to reflect the change.

Goals of game state:

To Inform

Purchased Game

After a game is purchased, the 'In Collection' label appears under the title to signify that the game has been added to the user's collection and is ready to be played.

Design Priority

Each state takes a different priority from the other. When states are in conflicting situations, the 'In Collection' label on the very left takes the highest priority, followed by the discount percentage, 'New', and 'Coming soon' label.

Impact

Tile System Simplifications

  • Displaying add-ons in consistent form factor and context
  • Distinguishing games and add-ons through different tile size
  • Removing the add-on badge
  • Removing the original strike-through price
