Overview

Introduction

EA is one of the largest gaming companies in the world, including games such as Apex Legends, The Sims 4, and It Takes Two. During my 4 month internship, I worked on the EA App, a PC platform for players to download, update, and manage their games. As well as to connect with other players across platforms.

Project

As a UX Design Intern, I discovered problems, created new game states and simplified the game tile system. Throughout the process, I worked with my Manager Dave, gaining experience presenting, building rationale, and practicing product thinking.

TL;DR

Problem

Through a design thinking workshop and looking at past user research studies, we identified three problems with the Game Tiles in the EA app.

1. Tiles are being presented at the same level of importance.

2. Cluttered discounted price label in a game tile

2. Inconsistent Add-on tile components throughout the application

Design Space

How might we draw attention to specific tiles in a discovery setting?
How might we simply the design system? How might distinguish Add-on tiles?

Solution

With the updated design of the game tiles, players are able to understand the different states of a game through labels, distinguish game tiles and add-ons through different tile sizes and view the home page comfortably through simplifying components .

Context

EA Application

The EA App is a windows application for players to download, update and manage their games. It provides the ability to connect with friends and integrate with external applications such as discord.

Throughout the application, game tiles can be found displaying key information such as the image of the game, price, and membership type.

Design Process

Audit/User research

Pt 1. Problem Identification

To familiarize myself with game tiles and application, I conducted an audit to identify the different types of tiles, read through UXR(User Experience Research) reports and briefly looked at competitors application.

The most interesting user research was understanding the user journey of a player and the archetypes' motivations.

After the research, I sorted the tiles into groups(user journey, location, tile size and shape, archetype motivations) to identify potential inconsistencies and gaps.

Pt 2. Problem prioritization

Along with my manager, the problems we identified were sorted and organized from highest impact to least effort to determine which problems to focus on.

Focus Areas

  1. How might we present different game states?
  2. How might we place price and discounts more effectively?
  3. How might we re-imagine a consistent Add-on tile design?

Competitive Analysis

With the problem space in mind, I wanted to understand how other competitors presented different states and the copy used, as well as how tiles are differentiated between games and DLC(game add-ons).

Although having category defined pages can prevent clustering of components within a game tile, the EA app does not benefit from adding pages because it has limited filtering capabilities and a lower quantity of games compared to other applications.

Design Ideas

Design Considerations

Copy Ideas

Prior to creating the visual design, we ideated on the possible states that could be included in a game tile. We looked through the user journey of a player, states used by competitor's and states that are in the process of development to determine a list of possible ideas.

After discussing with my Manager, the game states we chose to focus on were: Coming soon, Pre-orderable, New, Discount percentage (ex. -30%), and In-collection.

Interface Design

Components from the existing design system that we want to leverage.

Information Hierarchy

Although the position of the badge and the secondary text is fixed, there is some flexibility on where we want to place the tag.

We decided to further explore options with the label postioned before the title. Compared to the other placements, this design is hierarchically separated from the other label and does not create any awkward visual separation when the title text wraps.

Visual Hierarchy

We determined that the discount state would continue to lead in visual hierarchy because sales is the main driver of revenue. Followed by the 'New' state, we learned from user interviews and competitive analysis that this state is a key motivator for players. Lastly, we have the rest of the states incorporating a muted visual, and the 'Add-on' indicator that is differentiated slightly.

Game States Final Design

Brief

Currently, game titles are being presented at the same level of importance/hierarchy. This creates a design opportunity to draw increased attention to specific titles in a discovery setting that occupy a desirable or contextually useful status for the user.

What is a game state?

A certain stage within the life cycle of a game product. The game state that we have designed are split into two groups.

Success criteria

  1. Create a clear distinction between tiles with states and those without
  2. Create a clear distinction between game tiles and add-ons
  3. Reduce complexity within the game tile system
  4. Leverage existing design components

User Persona

Bob is a casual gamer that interacts with the EA application once in a while to access is games or to purchase and download new ones. His motivations are accessing new games or content, saving money, and having ownership of games that are part of an ongoing collection.

User Flow

Goals of game state:

To Attract

When Bob enters the EA App, he immediately notices the green neon labels in the game tile. In the landing page, there are 4 different labels.

Unreleased Game

Prior to the release of a game, there are two states: Coming soon and Pre-orderable.

Games that are unreleased are denoted with a 'Coming soon' label and if they are orderable, a price tag is included in addition to the label.

New Game

When a game is released, the game tile would include the 'New' label to notify and attract players.

Existing Game

On special occasions and holidays, EA offers games at a discounted price. During this time, selected games will have a discounted percentage to reflect the change.

Goals of game state:

To Inform

Purchased Game

After a game is purchased, an 'In Collection' label is located under the tile to signify that the game has been added to their collection and is ready to be played.

Design Priority

Each state takes different priority from each other. When states are in conflicting situations, the 'In Collection' label on the very left takes the highest priority followed by the discount percentage, 'New', and 'Coming soon' label.

Tile System Simplifications

  1. Displaying add-ons in consistent form factor and in context
  2. Distinguishing games and add-ons through different tile size
  3. Removing the add-on badge
  4. Removing the original strikethrough price
